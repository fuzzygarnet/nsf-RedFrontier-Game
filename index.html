Welcome to Red Frontier

The year is 2100, and humanity is diving headfirst into a new era of exploration—this time, beyond Earth. Mars is the next big frontier, and two rival companies have set up shop on the Red Planet. Their colonies are just getting started, with a handful of scientists, some food, water, medical supplies, and a stash of resources they've already mined. The goal? To turn these basic outposts into thriving, self-sufficient colonies that can support everyday people. To get there, they'll need to mine resources, research groundbreaking technologies, and build all the facilities a growing colony needs. But it’s not all cutthroat competition—this is a friendly race. The companies can trade resources to give each other a hand when needed. In the end, the first to create a fully self-sustaining colony wins the ultimate cash prize—and the bragging rights to go with it. Let the race to colonize Mars begin!

Instructions

  • Objective: The goal of the game is to be the first to grow your colony’s population to 500.
  • Starting Units: You begin with one miner unit on the surface of Mars.
  • Actions per Turn: Each turn, you can either: - Move your miner unit to an adjacent tile, or - Mine a tile for resources.
  • Resource Mining: When you mine a tile, you will receive a random amount of the following resources: - Water, - Sulfur, - Basalt, - Regolith.
  • Using Resources: Use the resources you gather to: - Purchase more miner units, - Construct buildings that will help your colony grow, - Trade with your opponent to acquire the resources you need.
  • Colony Management: To purchase new units or buildings, click on your base colony to open the managbent menu.
  • Ending Your Turn: To end your turn, you must take action with all the miner units currently on the board. This can be either moving thb or mining.
  • Technology Research: Research new technologies to unlock advanced buildings that help grow your colony’s population. - To access the research tree, click on the science icon. - Complete one technology in a branch to unlock further technologies in that branch.
  • Reducing Research Time: Speed up research by either: - Purchasing more technologies, - Constructing facilities that boost your research capabilities.
  • Learn More: For additional details on gameplay and mechanics, consult the Red-Frontier-Opedia, which contains all the information you need to succeed.

    Advanced Radiation Protection

    Protecting Martian settlers from harmful cosmic and solar radiation is vital for their safety. Several techniques utilize local materials to create effective shields. Piling 2 meters of regolith over habitats provides natural radiation protection, while basalt bricks and sulfur concrete offer durable, locally sourced building materials with shielding properties. Water, integrated into habitat walls, serves as both a radiation barrier and essential storage. Additionally, sintered regolith blocks provide sturdy, protective structures. By combining these approaches, Martian colonies can ensure safe and sustainable living spaces.

    Sintering

    Sintering is a process that transforms powdered materials into solid, durable objects by applying heat or pressure without melting them completely. During sintering, particles fuse as atoms diffuse across their boundaries, creating a cohesive, strong structure. On Mars, sintering could revolutionize construction by turning Martian regolith into building materials for habitats and infrastructure. This method eliminates the need to transport supplies from Earth, making it a sustainable solution for establishing a human presence. Already used in metallurgy, ceramics, and 3D printing on Earth, sintering’s adaptability makes it an essential tool for leveraging local resources on the Red Planet.
    Benefits: Decrease amount of resources needed for buildings

    Basalt

    Heat it, extrude it, and this rock becomes lightweight, strong, and corrosion-resistant—perfect for building habitats, protective domes, and reinforced infrastructure. Its adaptability shines in 3D printing, where processed basalt becomes the raw material for crafting complex structures on-site, cutting down on costly imports from Earth.

    But basalt’s benefits go even further. It’s the source of stone wool, a superb thermal insulator to keep habitats cozy, and its density acts as a natural shield against cosmic radiation. From bricks and tiles to roadways and landing pads, basalt is the go-to material for building a colony that’s built to last. With basalt, Martian settlers can truly shape their future from the planet’s own foundation.

    Regolith

    Regolith is more than debris—it’s the loose layer of dust, sand, and broken rocks covering Mars’ bedrock. With sintering, it can be hardened into bricks or tiles for habitats and infrastructure. Mix it with sulfur, and you’ve got recyclable concrete that’s perfect for Mars’ unique conditions. Its abundance also makes it ideal for radiation shields, offering vital protection from cosmic rays in the planet’s thin atmosphere.

    But regolith’s potential doesn’t stop at construction. Oxygen locked in its oxides can be extracted for breathable air, while metals like iron and aluminum can be refined for tools and structures. Even its perchlorate-rich soil, with treatment and nutrients, could support crops, turning barren dust into fertile ground. By harnessing the versatility of regolith, Martian settlers can reduce Earth dependency and lay the foundation for a self-sufficient colony.

    Sulfur

    Abundant and adaptable, sulfur is the magic behind sulfur concrete, a water-free material made by combining molten sulfur with Martian regolith. Resistant to extreme temperatures, acids, and salts, this durable concrete is perfect for Mars’ harsh environment. Its rapid curing allows for quick, efficient building, making it a top choice for constructing habitats and infrastructure.

    What sets sulfur apart is its recyclability. Structures made from sulfur concrete can be reheated, melted, and reshaped, offering a flexible way to manage resources. The process is simple: heat sulfur, mix it with soil, and let it cool—streamlining construction in Mars’ challenging conditions. With sulfur as a key resource, colonists can build sustainably, reducing reliance on Earth and paving the way for a resilient Martian colony.

    Water

    Essential for life support, water sustains colonists through drinking, food preparation, hygiene, and agriculture, enabling a self-sufficient food supply. Water's utility also extends to infrastructure and safety. Its hydrogen content is a natural shield against cosmic radiation, making it ideal for habitat walls to protect against Mars’ harsh environment. Additionally, water plays a key role in thermal regulation systems, ensuring stable temperatures within habitats and equipment, and is crucial in processing materials like concrete for building. With Martian water confirmed in ice deposits and possibly underground reservoirs, its in-situ use is vital to reducing Earth dependency and securing the sustainability of human life on the Red Planet.
